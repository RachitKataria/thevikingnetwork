Why We Exist

The goal of the Viking Network is to create tight bonds between Lynbrook alumni in the network through meaningful conversations and events, while supporting each other and pooling the networkâ€™s collective wisdom and talents. We follow the One-Degree Rule: every person within the network is directly friends with every other person in the network. This ensures that everyone feels comfortable enough to engage actively in personal discussions and seek support.

What We Do

The Viking Network functions through a combination of midyear retreats, regional groups, and monthly meetups, along with active Slack and Facebook groups, to cultivate and strengthen deep bonds and relationships with one another. The midyear retreats, held during summer and winter break, will be an opportunity for members to catch up and build new connections.

Following a very successful Winter 2017 retreat, we look forward to our next event this summer! Members are also encouraged to remain in touch and meet up during the school year; to this end, we will have regional meetups twice a semester facilitated by associated point persons.

Finally, in the spirit of the One-Degree Rule, we will have monthly virtual meetings in randomized groups of four. Shuffling groups on a regular basis introduces greater diversity -- not only by distance or class but also by personal experiences and perspectives -- in the interactions members have with one another, fostering strong and long-lasting friendships.
